Drivers of the 2026 F1 season

Home Teams Race Game

Alpine Drivers

  • Pierre Gasly

    Now the clear veteran leader at Alpine, Gasly continues to bring his trademark consistency and podium-hunting energy to the French outfit. He remains the team’s anchor as they navigate the new 2026 technical regulations.

  • Franco Colapinto

    After making a splash as a mid-season substitute in 2024 and building his reputation at Williams, Colapinto has secured a full-time seat at Alpine. The young Argentine is known for his aggressive overtaking and quick adaptation to new machinery.

Cadillac Drivers

  • Sergio "Checo" Perez

    Bringing massive experience and a reputation as a "tyre whisperer," Perez leads the charge for the new American-backed Cadillac entry. His role is to provide stability and points-scoring consistency for the newcomer.

  • Valtteri Bottas

    After a stint at Sauber, the ten-time race winner brings his Mercedes-honed technical expertise to Cadillac. Known for his qualifying speed and professional demeanor, he forms half of a very veteran lineup for the team's debut.

Aston Martin Drivers

  • Fernando Alonso

    The ageless wonder of the grid, Alonso continues his pursuit of a third world title well into his 40s. His feedback is vital as Aston Martin begins its new, exclusive engine partnership with Honda for the 2026 season.

  • Lance Stroll

    A permanent fixture at the Silverstone-based team, Stroll remains a solid midfield contender capable of occasional brilliance in wet or chaotic conditions. He continues to partner with Alonso in one of the grid's most experienced duos.

Audi Drivers

  • Nico Hülkenberg

    The "Hulk" finally gets his shot with a full works team as Sauber transitions into Audi. His legendary qualifying pace and technical feedback make him the perfect pilot to lead Audi’s debut entry into Formula 1.

  • Gabriel Bortoleto

    The 2023 F3 Champion and 2024 F2 standout, Bortoleto is Audi’s big investment in the future. As a highly-rated rookie, he carries the hopes of Brazil back onto the F1 grid as he learns the ropes alongside Hülkenberg.

Ferrari Drivers

  • Charles Leclerc

    The "Prince of Monaco" remains the heart of Ferrari, continuing his quest to bring a championship back to Maranello. His blistering raw speed is the benchmark the rest of the grid watches during Saturday qualifying sessions.

  • Lewis Hamilton

    In what remains the biggest driver move in the sport's history, the seven-time World Champion continues his Ferrari journey. His presence has transformed the team’s atmosphere as he hunts for a record-breaking eighth title in red.

Haas Drivers

  • Esteban Ocon

    Moving over from Alpine, Ocon brings race-winning experience to the American squad. Known for his defensive tenacity and "never-give-up" attitude, he is tasked with leading Haas into their new era of technical partnership with Toyota (TGR).

  • Oliver Bearman

    Following his legendary 2024 debut as a teenager, "Ollie" is now a full-time fixture. The British prodigy is one of the most exciting young talents on the grid, carrying high expectations from the Ferrari Driver Academy.

Mclaren Drivers

  • Lando Norris

    Coming off his 2025 World Championship victory, Norris is now the man with the target on his back. He has evolved into a complete race-winner and the definitive leader of the papaya-clad squad.

  • Oscar Piastri

    The calmest man in F1, Piastri continues to push his teammate to the limit. His clinical precision and rapid development have made him a regular podium finisher and a threat for the title in his own right.

Mercedes Drivers

  • George Russell

    Having stepped into the role of team leader following Hamilton's departure, Russell is the face of the Silver Arrows. He is the primary hope for Mercedes to return to their dominant ways under the 2026 engine rules.

  • Kimi Antonelli

    The Italian sensation made the jump to F1 with immense hype. As one of the youngest drivers on the grid, his "lightning-fast" reputation is being put to the test as he learns the intricacies of the Mercedes W17.

RB Drivers

  • Liam Lawson

    After years of waiting in the wings, Lawson has firmly established himself as a full-time starter. The New Zealander is known for his "no-nonsense" racing style and ability to extract every bit of pace from the car.

  • Arvid Lindblad

    The latest star from the Red Bull Junior Team, Lindblad makes his debut as one of the youngest rookies in history. He represents the next generation of talent being groomed for a future Red Bull seat.

Red Bull Drivers

  • Max Verstappen

    The dominant force of the 2020s, Verstappen remains the benchmark for the entire grid. Even with the move to Red Bull-Ford powertrains, he is the favorite for the title every time he suits up.

  • Isack Hadjar

    After a brilliant rookie year at RB, Hadjar has been promoted to the "big team." The Frenchman is known for his aggressive spirit and raw pace, and he now faces the ultimate test: being Max Verstappen's teammate.

Williams Drivers

  • Alexander Albon

    The cornerstone of the Williams rebuild, Albon continues to lead the team with his exceptional tyre management and tactical intelligence. He has become a hero at Grove for his ability to drag the car into the points

  • Carlos Sainz

    Bringing "Smooth Operator" energy to Williams, Sainz forms one of the strongest pairings on the grid. His technical knowledge and race craft are instrumental in Williams' push to climb back toward the front of the pack.
